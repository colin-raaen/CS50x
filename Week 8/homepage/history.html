History

US Open History

The US Open has been played on a variety of surfaces throughout its history.

Beginnings

The US Open was first played in 1881 on grass courts at the Newport Casino in Newport, Rhode Island. The tournament moved to several other venues before settling in Flushing Meadows, New York in 1978.

Surface Changes

The US Open has been played on several different surfaces throughout its history. The tournament was played on grass until 1974, when it switched to clay. In 1978, it switched to hard courts.

Recent History

The US Open has seen many memorable moments in recent history, including Serena Williams' emotional victory in 2020 and Novak Djokovic's historic win in 2021.
